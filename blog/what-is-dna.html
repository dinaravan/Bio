What is DNA?

Published: May 2025

DNA, or deoxyribonucleic acid, is the molecular blueprint for all living organisms. It carries the instructions necessary for life and is passed down from one generation to the next.

In this post, we'll explore the structure of DNA, how it replicates, and why it's so fundamental to biology and medicine.

The Double Helix

The famous double-helix shape was discovered by Watson and Crick in 1953. DNA is composed of four types of nucleotides: Adenine (A), Thymine (T), Cytosine (C), and Guanine (G). These base pairs connect in specific ways: A pairs with T, and C pairs with G.

Why DNA Matters

Understanding DNA has allowed scientists to identify genetic disorders, solve crimes, and even develop personalized medicine. It's one of the most powerful discoveries in science.
